Problem 1

Calculations

We first want to assume a model in which all anthropogenic forcings are placed into the atmosphere, which we define as follows: \begin{equation} \frac{\textrm{d}}{\textrm{d}t}\left[\left(C_a \circ \delta^{13}C_{a}\right)(t)\right] = F(t)\delta^{13}C_a, \end{equation} where $\left(C_a \circ \delta^{13}C_{a}\right)(t)$ is the weighted product of total carbon in a resevoir denoted by the subscript and the isotope for the same resevoir. Here, we define $F$ as the emission forcing given by: \begin{equation} F(t) = \left(\frac{2(C_T - \Delta t)}{\Delta t} + r_0\right)t + r_0, \end{equation} which provides: \begin{equation} \widehat{F}(t) = \int\text{d}F = \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{2(C_T - \Delta t)}{\Delta t} + r_0\right)\tilde{t}^2 + r_0\tilde{t}, \end{equation} for $C_T$ the total emissions over the period $\Delta t$ (i.e., 1950 to 2020), $r_0$ the initial rate of emission (1 [Gt y$^{-1}$]), and $\tilde{t} = t - 1950$. This is simply the primitive of the linear increase described in the problem, modified to make the rate self-contained with the total emissions discussed in the problem. Specifically, we change $C_T$, which we do in the top panel (solid and dashed lines) of Fig. 1, to induce different emission scenarios. The solid line scenario is for $C_T = 440$ [Gt y$^{-1}$] and the dashed line is for $C_T = 881 - 612 = 269$ [Gt y$^{-1}$].

Now, we can define the mixing relationship used to calculate the isotope values by integrating the first equation, using Separation of Variables to get: \begin{equation} \int_{t'\in\left[t_0,t\right]} \text{d}\left[\left(C_a \circ \delta^{13}C_{a}\right)(t')\right] = \int_{t_0}^t \text{d}t' \text{F}(t')\delta^{13}C_a, \end{equation} which gives: \begin{equation} C_a(t)\delta^{13}C_a(t) = C_a(t_0)\delta^{13}C_a(t_0) + \widehat{F}(t)\delta^{13}C_{p}, \end{equation} where $\delta^{13}C_p$ is the constant isotope value for anthropogenic emissions and we assume $t_0=1950$ is at pre-industrial levels. We present the solutions for the two different emission scenarios in the middle plot of Fig. 1 (solid and dashed).

Next, we define a piece-wise forcing function for $\Delta^{14}C$ of the form: \begin{equation} \Delta^{14}C_s(t) = \begin{cases} -20 & 1950 \le t < 1955 \\ -20 + 75 \tilde{t} & 1955 \le t < 1965 \\ 730 & 1965 \le t \end{cases} \end{equation} This is based on analysis and discussion from Graven et al. (2017). We then correct for decay by: \begin{equation} \Delta^{14}C(t) = \Delta^{14}C_s(t)\exp\left[\lambda\left(t_p - \widehat{t}\right)\right], \end{equation} where $t_p$ is the data of production (starting in 1955 and ending in 1965), $\widehat{t} = t - 1955$, and $\lambda = \ln 2 / 5730$ [y$^{-1}$]. We plot $\Delta^{14}C(t)$ in the bottom plot of Fig. 1.
