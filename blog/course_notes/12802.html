12.802: Small-Scale Ocean Circulation

Syllabus

The list of topics covered in this course are as follows:

  1. Waves
    • General wave concepts
      1. Amplitude, phase, wavenumber, frequency, phase velocity
      2. Stationary phase, group velocity, energy flux
      3. Slowly varying media: conservation of crests, ray theory, wave action conservation
      4. Review of governing equations
    • Surface gravity waves
      1. Potential theory
      2. Dispersion relation, long and short waves
      3. Stokes drift
    • Internal gravity waves
      1. unbounded domain, constant N, f
      2. bounded domain, vertical modes
      3. Constant N, reflection from slope
      4. Garrett-Munk spectrum
    • Long waves with rotation, homogeneous fluid
      1. Poincar√©, Kelvin and Rossby waves
      2. Topographic waves, coastal-trapped waves
      3. Equatorial waves
      4. Waves and transient ocean circulations
      5. Spin up and spin down
  2. Instabilities
    • General concepts of linear instability
    • Convective instability
    • Kelvin-Helmholtz instability
    • Continuous flows: Rayleigh and Kelvin Helmholtz problems
    • Barotropic instability
    • Baroclinic instability (Eady Model, Phillips Model)
    • Submesoscale instabilities
  3. Small-scale turbulence
    • Homogeneous turbulence: TKE equation (production, dissipation, etc.), Kolmogorov scaling, inertial range, spectral description, scalars
    • Stratified turbulence: Flux Richardson number, Ozmidov scale, mixing efficiency. The connection to microstructure measurements
  4. Other small-scale flows
    • Wave-vortex interactions / separation
    • Waves in a mean flow
    • Turbulent boundary layers: Law of the Wall, Stokes-Ekman Problem
    • The entrainment assumption: plumes, downslope flows (parameterized effects of stratified turbulence)
    • Single-layer hydraulic control and jumps (a nonlinear wave problem)
    • Coastal upwelling/downwelling
    • Shelf-break front instability
    • Ray tracing
